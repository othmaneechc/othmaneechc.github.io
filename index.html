About Me

I’m Othmane Echchabi, an incoming M.Sc. student in Computer Science at McGill University / Mila Québec AI Institute. Current Projects

Here are a selection of my recent projects spanning satellite‑based environmental monitoring, machine learning for mobility and carbon footprint prediction, and AI‑driven computer vision applications.

Tracking Progress Towards SDG6

Assessed piped water and sewage access in 50 African countries using vision transformers on satellite imagery (97% accuracy), and built a comprehensive dataset to monitor Sustainable Development Goal 6.

Carbon Clever & Trip Mode Prediction

Developed a transformer‑based self‑supervised model achieving 92% accuracy on GeoLife and 95% on MOBIS for trip‑mode identification, and designed CarbonClever to help users reduce their individual carbon footprints.

Monitoring Spartina Alterniflora

Applied self‑supervised learning with ViTs to identify and map the distribution of saltmarsh cordgrass from coastal wetland imagery, presented at Duke’s Climate+ Symposium.

Assessing Climate Change Risk

Created a geospatial database in ArcGIS and used NLP on 1,000+ articles to map saltwater intrusion and sea level rise risks for rural coastal plain communities.

Yancheng Wetlands Degradation

Conducted a 25‑year spatiotemporal NDVI analysis of Yancheng coastal wetlands degradation and developed a state‑of‑the‑art wetland segmentation model.

DigCai Database

Collaborated as an editor on the first release of DigCai v4.0.0: a TEI-XML marked-up corpus of Cairo’s urban development articles (1828–1914) from the Egyptian Government Gazette, in Arabic and Ottoman Turkish, supported by an NEH grant.
